How much fuel does a rocket need?

Getting a rocket into orbit is no easy matter. The [rocket] science is explored in this blog post which describes the conditions needed to raise the rocket's velocity to the point where it can "[slip] the surly bonds of earth" and establish an orbit.

One way of thinking about the problem is the following:

  • The payload (the important part of the craft) has a mass, m
  • To accelerate mass m to the required velocity, some amount of fuel, x, is needed
  • However, launching with an additional amount of fuel x on board brings the total rocket mass to m + x which is greater than the original mass, m, used in the calculation
  • To accelerate additional mass x, some additional amount of fuel, y, is needed. This brings the total rocket mass to m + x + y
  • To accelerate additional mass y, even more fuel, z, is needed
  • ... and so on

Write a recursive solution to calculate the amount of fuel required to place into orbit a body of the given mass. Assume that the amount of fuel necessary to accelerate a mass of m to the required velocity is given by the equation f = m / 3 - 2. Any value of f < 0 should be set to zero.

Example calculation:

Recursions.calculateFuel(1000);
// fuel required to lift the original payload of 1000
1000 / 3 - 2 = 333 - 2 = 331
// fuel required to lift that additional 331 units of fuel
331 / 3 - 2 = 110 - 2 = 108
// fuel required to lift that additional 108 units of fuel
108 / 3 - 2 = 36 - 2 = 34
// fuel required to lift that additional 34 units of fuel
34 / 3 - 2 = 11 - 2 = 9
// fuel required to lift that additional 9 units of fuel
9 / 3 - 2 = 3 - 2 = 1
// fuel required to lift that additional 1 unit of fuel
1 / 3 - 2 = 0 - 2 = -2 -> 0
// no additional fuel needed so calculate the total
returns 483 // (331 + 108 + 34 + 9 + 1 + 0)
